Luci Atkins

Freelance Photographer

My Story

Ever since I was a child I had an interest in cameras, from the toy camera that would change images when you clicked the shutter, to an old poloroid camera my Father used to own. Some would say it was my calling but for me it was just something I loved to do and I was lucky enough to make it my profession.

I orignally started out posting my shots online and sharing them with friends but as my work began to get more and more attention I found myself being scouted for work pretty regularly and the jobs ranged greatly from small events to big magazines wanting to use my photos. I was astounded at how much people liked my photos so I decided I would make this my career and the rest is history.

My Work

Although I started out with many small events such as birthday parties and christenings I steadily began to climb the ranks if you will. I began to get bigger and bigger gigs such as weddings, which are a big deal in my opinion, to jobs for smaller magazines and newspapers until I got my big break in the form of The New York Times! This is my greatest achievement to date and something I am very proud of.

I had worked for many different newspapers in Ireland in the past but to get a call from The New York Times was mind boggling and I was pretty stunned. Having my photos in probably the most famous newspaper in the world was something I could hardly believe.
